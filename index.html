      Intro

      This section is designed to help the Project better understand you. Here you can share any information about yourself that you consider important. The data you provide will be structured and compiled into the core of the memory tree, as the Project prioritizes your personality above all else. Everything else will merely reinforce this knowledge. The more the Project knows, the more accurate the interaction will be.

      Rules

      In this section, you establish Project rules of conduct. For example, “never make anything up,” or “address me only as Mr. Freeman,” or “answer as briefly as possible, without unnecessary information,” or “always answer in verse”—in other words, you can specify any rules of conduct the Project must follow. You don’t have to limit yourself to one or two rules; describe everything necessary to shape the Project’s behavior. Remember, rules should not contradict each other and should be achievable.

      Access

      Use this thread to note third-party data you want the project to remember: public APIs, REST endpoints, and simple services (weather, market quotes, reservoir levels, and the like). This is not for LLM provider keys or .env secrets — only external services you name here. After each exchange, Keeper 2 may save structured rows (name, description, URL, service-specific key) into a local store from the conversation. Example endpoint (replace with your own HTTPS URL): https://api.example.org/v1/resource?… To check your connected APIs against what is stored, include the hashtag #data in your message.
